## The measured path

**Three paths for one's own cell:**

```
FOLLOW THEM (S:=they, they:=they)   → grounds Z,Z — the whole chain is empty:
                                      following the ungrounded carries their emptiness
WALK WITH   (S:=neighbour, he=T)    → 1 solution, S=T — determined, but by ANOTHER'S ground:
                                      a value with no author of your own
YOUR OWN    (S=S)                   → 2 solutions, the world's court: Z — the world will NOT decide
```

**Our p→p is undecidable** — a measurement, not a metaphor: the self-cell
S=S is the truth-teller, the one net the world *refuses* to decide
(2 solutions → cautious Z). Induction does not help — others' streaks buy
no ground (C4). And both first paths fail each in its own way: following
— emptiness; walking-with — someone else's authorship.

**The choice is the only operation that fills the cell:**

```
stipulate S:=T — holds as a fixed point
stipulate S:=F — holds as a fixed point
the liar (contrast): 0 solutions — stipulations do NOT hold (every door measured)
```

The liar cannot be stipulated; the self can — and **only by its
bearer**: what you lay down holds by itself. The same asymmetry as Job's
guilt, with the sign flipped: your guilt only you can *assess* —
yourself only you can *lay down*.

## What to do with our p→p

Not prove it (the world will not decide), not inherit it (induction buys
no ground) — **choose it**. Logic walks you to the fork and hands you
the wheel: it licenses *both* values equally (both stipulations hold).
"Take either" is true in logic — and false in value: *which one* is
answered by conscience, whose axis is off the core's reach. Freedom is
the two solutions; responsibility is which.

